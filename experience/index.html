Experience

Qualitative

Macmillan Learning

Since March 2018, I have worked as a senior content standards project manager, focusing on Macmillan’s EPUB content. I manage the SCSS, schema, and EPUB development projects, helping to ensure that our content adheres to industry standards for accessibility, quality, and readability.

In order to support the quality of our products, I assist in developing our Validator tool which checks against industry standards (Ace accessibility validator, HTML Validator, CSS Jigsaw Validator, and custom rules), troubleshoot EPUB issues, and develop workflows for EPUB products that feed directly into our courseware programs.

I updated Macmillan Learning’s EPUB accessibility standards to qualify for Benetech’s Global Certified Accessible credential starting for all our titles from 2019 onward. Additionally, representing Macmillan, I am currently working on a forthcoming IEEE standard and acted as the lead editor for the Web Publications Use Cases and Requirements, a note from the Publishing Working Group at the W3C.

  • Macmillan EPUB CSS and Standard CSS Themes
  • Accessible EPUB Specification and Spec EPUB
  • Extended Description rollout
  • Macmillan Schema
  • Macmillan Validator

Brill

From February 2017 to March 2018, I worked as a project manager for major reference works at Brill Academic Publisher. I managed a variety of online reference works, balancing deadlines and interacting with vendors, copyeditors, outside editorial boards, and internal acquisitions editors. I streamlined and improved the XML-based workflows for these projects. I also developed CSS- and HTML-based improvements on the reference works website with the platform team. Additionally, I consulted on EPUB workflows.

F+W Media

From September 2013 to the end of January 2017, I worked at F+W Media, managing the ebook workflow and developing an CMS-based XML-first workflow for the Adams Media group, which was later sold to Simon & Schuster. The CMS allowed us to leverage our content like never before, using one source for various outputs: InDesign, HTML, EPUB, and direct XML feeds for content licensing. The work at F+W allowed me to do a great deal of creative problem solving, collaborating and discussing with my colleagues, and overall making a more efficient and optimal workflow for the future.

  • Crimson Romance, CMS ebook workflow

Strangelet Press

From May 2012 to the end of 2016, I and five colleagues from Emerson College ran a science fiction/fantasy literary magazine called Strangelet. It involved us wearing a lot of different hats. At one point, I developed a new business plan for the press. While the editors handled the words, I handled the design: the print books, the ebooks, and the website.

  • Strangelet series, book design, CSS and XHTML
  • Strangelet Press website

Freelance

Since 2013, I have worked a variety of freelance projects. I handled the ebook development for Litweaver for two years, creating a template for their series of young adult titles. I have created a variety of titles for Wise Ink Press, among them an enhanced children’s ebook (with music). I also worked with Library For All, a nonprofit developing digital libraries to expand accessibility to ebooks in the developing world. Additionally, I do freelance web design and development.

  • Rice and Rocks, fixed-layout children’s book
  • Litweaver Library, ebook development
  • Your Dream Cakes, website

Conferences

In 2019, my colleague Betsy Granger and I presented at Ebookcraft in Toronto. Our presentation was on developing a grid-like layout for Macmillan Learning’s EPUBs. We also presented on other options we considered.

In 2017, I was on a panel on repurposing content at Tech Forum in Toronto. I related my experiences with repurposing digital content, leveraging the XML-based CMS workflow of Adams Media at F+W Media.
