Experience, qualitative

Philosophy

I have always strived to be efficient, to make the little things automatic and out of the way. Whether writing Python scripts to automate tasks or planning meals weeks in advance, I always like to make more space to create.

Brill

In February 2017, I started as project manager of reference works for Brill, an academic publisher.

F+W Media

From September 2013 to the end of January 2017, I worked at F+W Media, managing the ebook workflow and developing an CMS-based XML-first workflow for the Adams Media group, which was later sold to Simon & Schuster. The CMS allowed us to leverage our content like never before, using one source for various outputs: InDesign, HTML, EPUB, and direct XML feeds for content licensing. The work at F+W allowed me to do a great deal of creative problem solving, collaborating and discussing with my colleagues, and overall making a more efficient and optimal workflow for the future.

  • Crimson Romance, CMS ebook workflow

Strangelet Press

From May 2012 to the end of 2016, I and some colleagues from Emerson College ran a science fiction/fantasy literary magazine called Strangelet. It involved us wearing a lot of different hats, even at one point for me, coming up with a new business plan for the press. While the editors handled the words, I handled the design: the print books, the ebooks, and the website.

  • Strangelet series, book design, CSS and XHTML
  • Strangelet Press website

Freelance

Since 2013, I have worked a variety of freelance projects. I handled the ebook development for Litweaver for two years, creating a template for their series of young adult titles. I have created a variety of titles for Wise Ink Press, among them an enhanced children’s ebook (with music). Additionally, I do freelance web design and development.

  • Rice and Rocks, fixed-layout children’s book
  • Litweaver Library, ebook development
  • Your Dream Cakes, website

Education

I have a Master of Arts in Publishing and Writing from Emerson College and a Bachelor of Arts in English from the University of Florida. I have independently been studying web design and development since I started in publishing. Since so much of ebook development is HTML and CSS, it is a natural next step. Publishing exists not just in ink, but in pixels.

  • GradeYouLater, single page web application
  • Redivider, literary magazine
  • Beowulf, book design
  • Once Magazine

Other interests

I am writing a fantasy novel, I like to play guitar, and I like cooking.
